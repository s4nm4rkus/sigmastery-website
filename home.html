What is Sigma Notation?

Sigma notation is a mathematical notation used to represent the sum of a series of numbers. It is denoted by the Greek letter sigma (Σ), which stands for ‘sum.’

History of Sigma Notation

Sigma notation was denoted by the Greek letter Σ, was introduced by Swiss mathematician Leonhard Euler in the 18th century. Euler developed this notation to succinctly represent sums of mathematical series. The symbol Σ represents the phrase “sum of,” and it allows mathematicians to express series concisely.
